We identify and follow individual giant molecular clouds in high-resolution
simulations of Milky-Way-mass galaxies, constructing cloud-evolution trees that
capture mergers, fragmentation, and full cloud life cycles from formation through
disruption. Across our suite, GMCs form continuously from gas accretion and
gravitational collapse, and disperse on timescales of a few free-fall times once
stellar feedback dominates.

We find that local stellar feedback is the principal driver of cloud destruction
and of the cloud-scale star-formation efficiency, while galactic-scale processes
modulate cloud assembly and survival. The integrated efficiency depends weakly on
environment and clusters around a few percent. We discuss implications for
galactic-scale subgrid models that depend on a single global star-formation
efficiency.
