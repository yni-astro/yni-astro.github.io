I am Yang Ni, a PhD candidate in the Department of Astronomy at Tsinghua University, working with Prof. Xuening Bai. I expect to defend in 2027. Before Tsinghua I received my B.S. in Astronomy from Nanjing University (Magna Cum Laude, 2022), where I worked with Prof. Bin Luo on high-energy observations.

My research uses numerical simulations to bridge galactic and protostellar scales, asking how the gas reservoirs of galaxies give rise to the stars and planets we observe today. I work primarily with the GIZMO code and have contributed to its M1 radiative-transfer module.

Research interests

Protoplanetary disks & planet formation

Early-stage evolution of self-gravitating disks; direct formation of gas giants via disk fragmentation in 3D radiation hydrodynamics.

Giant molecular clouds & star formation

The life cycle of GMCs in Milky-Way-mass galaxies, the role of stellar feedback, and how galactic-scale physics regulates cloud-scale star formation efficiency.

Numerical methods

Radiation hydrodynamics, M1 closure schemes, large-scale parallel simulations on HPC systems.
