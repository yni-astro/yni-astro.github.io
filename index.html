I am a PhD candidate in astronomy at Tsinghua University, advised by Prof. Xuening Bai (expected to defend in 2027). My research uses (radiation-)hydrodynamic simulations to study how stars and planets are born — from the giant molecular clouds that birth star clusters down to the protoplanetary disks where gas giants emerge.

Currently I focus on the early-stage evolution of self-gravitating protoplanetary disks and on the life cycle of giant molecular clouds in galaxy-scale simulations. I work primarily with the GIZMO code and am a developer of its M1 radiative-transfer module. Before Tsinghua I received my B.S. in Astronomy from Nanjing University (Magna Cum Laude, 2022), where I worked with Prof. Bin Luo on high-energy observations.

Research interests

  • Protoplanetary disks & planet formation: early-stage evolution of self-gravitating disks; direct formation of gas giants via disk fragmentation in 3D radiation hydrodynamics
  • Giant molecular clouds & star formation: the life cycle of GMCs in Milky-Way-mass galaxies; the role of stellar feedback; how galactic-scale physics regulates cloud-scale star-formation efficiency
  • Numerical methods: radiation hydrodynamics; M1 closure schemes; large-scale parallel simulations on HPC systems
